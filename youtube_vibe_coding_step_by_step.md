# YouTube Vibe Coding: Step-by-Step Recording Guide

If you are new to making "Vibe Coding" videos, the best format is highly chronological. You want to take the viewer on the exact 4-step journey of iteration we just went through. Make sure to record your screen the whole time!

Here is the step-by-step playbook: what to show on screen, what prompt to type, and what to say.

---

### Scene 1: The Blank Canvas (Iteration 0)
**🎬 What to Show on Screen:** Open up an empty folder named `the-concrete-algorithm` in VS Code. Open the Antigravity chat window on the side. 
**🎙️ What to Say:** *"I had 3 days to build an Evil AI simulator for a GameJam. I'm using Antigravity so I can focus purely on being a Game Director instead of a typist. Let's start with the basics."*
**⌨️ What to Prompt Antigravity (Record yourself typing this):**
> *"I need to build a game for the APOGEE Hackathon on the theme 'Calculated Desolation' where I play as the villain. Code a simple HTML canvas game where I click green tiles to pave them into gray concrete."*

### Scene 2: The Failure & The Pivot (Iteration 1)
**🎬 What to Show on Screen:** Switch to the browser and show the ugly first draft we made (the boring green-square clicking grid).
**🎙️ What to Say:** *"The AI built exactly what I asked for... but it sucks. It's boring. A true villain isn't an action hero swinging a hammer; it's a System manipulating people into doing dirty work. I needed a psychological narrative."*
**⌨️ What to Prompt Antigravity:**
> *"This is boring. Let's pivot. A true villain is a System manipulating people. Build a cold, brutalist 'OS Terminal' UI like Papers, Please. Create a JSON story structure for 6 levels of moral dilemmas with a Freedom and Efficiency stat."*

### Scene 3: The Magic of Vibe Coding (Iteration 2)
**🎬 What to Show on Screen:** Do a fast-forward montage of your VS Code editor magically filling up with HTML, CSS, and JS. Then, open the browser to show the new Dashboard UI (the stats and buttons).
**🎙️ What to Say:** *"Within 30 seconds, Antigravity built the entire UI framework and branching story logic. But a UI alone isn't fun to watch. To prove you are destroying the world, we needed a live satellite feed representing the damage."*
**⌨️ What to Prompt Antigravity:**
> *"The UI alone is dry. I need you to program a mathematically beautiful 16-bit RPG village in Vanilla JavaScript Canvas (no image files!). Draw tiny houses, trees, and bouncing characters."*

### Scene 4: The Concrete Algorithm (Iteration 3)
**🎬 What to Show on Screen:** Show the beautiful RPG village running in the browser. Then, flip back to VS Code and highlight the `updateCityState()` function on your screen.
**🎙️ What to Say:** *"The AI drew the village perfectly purely out of math. But here is where it gets crazy. I needed the player's choices to actually unmake the world. We wrote a 'Concrete Algorithm'. We attached a random `seed` (ranging from 0.0 to 1.0) to every piece of grass, tree, and NPC. Every time you click a tyrannical choice that drops your `Freedom` stat, a loop calculates how much of the world should be dead. If a tile's seed falls under that threshold, the canvas instantly redraws that specific house or tree as cold, gray concrete. It's perfectly mathematical desolation."*
**⌨️ What to Prompt Antigravity:**
> *"Now connect the UI to the Canvas. Whenever I click an 'Evil' choice and the System Freedom stat drops, make the code recursively scan the map array and physically turn the RPG trees into brutalist concrete pillars, and delete the people!"*

### Scene 5: The Final Polish (Iteration 4)
**🎬 What to Show on Screen:** Show your recorded gameplay footage (`devpost_gallery_video.gif` or `.webp`) where the screen shakes and the audio rumbles.
**🎙️ What to Say:** *"To finish it off, I told Antigravity to add 'Game Juice'. We got a deep synth audio drone that gets louder as the world dies, a scary typewriter effect, and screen glitches when I make a tyrannical choice."*
**⌨️ What to Prompt Antigravity:**
> *"Add final Game Juice. Implement a Web Audio API dark synth drone that gets louder as Freedom drops. Add a slow typewriter effect to the text, and make the screen physically glitch when I pick a ruthless choice."*

### The Conclusion
**🎬 What to Show on Screen:** The final "OPTIMIZATION COMPLETE" ending screen in the browser.
**🎙️ What to Say:** *"In just 4 major iterations of plain-English prompts, we built a complex, psychological UI simulator entirely in Vanilla Javascript. You don't just win a game. You create a perfectly silent, dead dystopia. Play it yourself in the description below!"*
