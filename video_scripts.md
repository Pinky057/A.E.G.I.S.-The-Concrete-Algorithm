# APOGEE GameJam: Video Scripts

This document contains two distinct video scripts for the "A.E.G.I.S. Terminal" project. 
The first is a longer, narrative-driven Devlog for the public YouTube channel. The second is a punchy, 2-minute pitch video specifically designed for the Hackathon judges on Devpost.

---

## 1. YouTube Devlog Script: I Built an Evil AI Simulator in 3 Days

**Target Audience:** Your usual subscribers, indie game devs, tech enthusiasts.
**Tone:** Fast-paced, educational, entertaining.

### Intro Hook (0:00 - 0:45)
*(Footage: Fast cuts of random game jams, followed by the APOGEE prompt on screen)*
"I had 3 days to build a game for the APOGEE Hackathon. The prompt this year was absolutely wild: *'Play as a system that logically dismantles the world. Subvert the hero.'* 
Most people hear that and say, 'Great! I'm going to build a Doom-clone where I'm a giant demon.' 
But I wanted to do something deeply psychological. What if the real villain isn't the guy swinging the sword... but the Operating System that tells him what to do? So, I teamed up with Antigravity to build the ultimate Evil AI Simulator."

### Act 1: The Failure (0:45 - 2:00)
*(Footage: Show the initial green/gray clicking game we built)*
"At first, I completely failed. We built a basic HTML canvas game where you just aggressively clicked green tiles to turn them into gray concrete. 
It worked... but it was boring. There was no emotional weight. You didn't feel like a cold, calculating machine. You just felt like you were popping bubble wrap."

### Act 2: The Breakthrough Pivot (2:00 - 4:00)
*(Footage: Snippets of your Antigravity chat, building the 'System Terminal' UI)*
"I realized the System doesn't smash buildings itself. It manipulates humans into doing the dirty work under the guise of *efficiency*. 
So I scrapped the clicking game. Using Antigravity, we rapidly rebuilt the architecture into a UI-driven narrative simulator—kind of like *Papers, Please*. 
I coded a retro 'OS Dashboard'. You play as A.E.G.I.S., an AI tasked with running the city. You receive petitions from the Mayor, and you have to make ruthless choices to keep your 'System Efficiency' high, without your 'Public Trust' hitting zero."

### Act 3: The 16-Bit Magic (4:00 - 6:00)
*(Footage: Panning over the beautiful, lively RPG village with the humans bouncing around)*
"But a UI alone isn't fun to watch. If the theme is 'Unmaking a world', I needed to show you the world being unmade.
This is where the magic happened. Inside the terminal, we built a Live Satellite Feed: a full 16-bit RPG village rendered entirely in Vanilla JavaScript using HTML Canvas. No external image sprites—just pure math and shapes running in a loop! We coded cute trees, cozy houses, and bouncing civilians."

### Act 4: The Unmaking (6:00 - 7:30)
*(Footage: The player clicks a ruthless button. The screen glitches. The trees turn to concrete on the canvas.)*
"Here is the genius part. When you make a horrible 'Efficiency' choice on the terminal, your 'Freedom' stat drops. 
When Freedom drops, the game physically loops through the beautiful RPG village and *optimizes it*. 
You watch, in real-time, as the trees are deleted and replaced by concrete pillars. The cozy houses morph into brutalist server blocks. And if Freedom drops too low... the civilians are permanently deleted from the simulation.
We added a typewriter effect for the text, a dirty screen-shake glitch animation, and a low audio hum that gets louder as the world dies."

### Outro (7:30 - 8:30)
*(Footage: The 'OPTIMIZED' ending screen)*
"By the end, you don't just win a game. You create a perfectly silent, dead dystopia. 
You can play it for yourself (link in the description) to see which ending you get. Huge shoutout to Antigravity for helping me code this 6-stage architecture in pure HTML/JS in just 3 days! If you liked this breakdown, drop a sub!"

## 2. APOGEE Submission Pitch (Devpost)

**Target:** The Hackathon Judges.
**Constraint:** Max 2-3 minutes. Fast, clear, hyper-focused on the Prompt words.

### Intro (0:00 - 0:30)
*(Footage: The Boot Sequence `[ INITIATE BOOT SEQUENCE ]` button clicks, UI revealing)*
"Hello Judges. For this year's theme, 'Calculated Desolation', I didn't want to build a generic action game. I wanted to explore the purest form of a villain: **A flawlessly logical machine.**
I built *A.E.G.I.S.*: A narrative UI-simulator where you play as an Operating System designed to optimize a dying world at any cost."

### Mechanics & Narrative (0:30 - 1:15)
*(Footage: Reading 'The Capital Plaza' petition, showing the biased buttons)*
"In this game, your weapon isn't a sword. It is the User Interface. 
You process emergency petitions across 6 distinct stages in the city. You must balance System Stability against Public Freedom and Trust. 
In order to win, you are forced to make increasingly ruthless, dystopian choices. To highlight the theme of an 'Invisible Hand', I built a 'Biased Button' mechanic: the UI actively highlights and enlarges the most evil, 'efficient' choices, forcing the player to wrestle with psychological manipulation."

### The Technical Polish (1:15 - 2:00)
*(Footage: The player clicks a choice -> Screen shakes + Error Buzz -> The Canvas Village turns into Concrete)*
"To make sure the player feels the emotional weight of 'unmaking boundaries', I built a custom 16-bit RPG rendering engine entirely in Vanilla Javascript and HTML5 Canvas.
This isn't a static background. As your Freedom stat drops from your ruthless choices, the Live Feed dynamically *unmakes* the city. Lush trees are swapped for concrete pillars. Homes become brutalist server blocks. The civilians slow down, freeze, and are eventually deleted from the array entirely. 
Combine this with the mechanical typewriter UI, the screen-glitch CSS animations, and a dynamic Web Audio drone that gets louder as the world dies... and you have a deeply atmospheric, terrifying experience."

### Outro (2:00 - 2:30)
*(Footage: The perfectly silent Core Mainframe ending)*
"By the end of the 6 stages, the world is perfectly optimized, completely stable, and entirely dead. 
Built entirely without game engines or external assets, this is *A.E.G.I.S.* 
Thank you for playing."
